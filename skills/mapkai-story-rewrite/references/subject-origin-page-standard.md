# MapKAI Subject Origin Page Standard

Use this reference when the user asks for a Subject Origin page, discipline origin page, field origin page, "学科起源", "领域起源", or a page explaining how a knowledge field emerged.

## Purpose

A Subject Origin page is not a short encyclopedia card and not a long academic essay. It should help readers understand:

- why a discipline emerged;
- what older question or older way of working became insufficient;
- what new way of seeing appeared;
- how the field later developed into branches.

The page should show a discipline as a historical or conceptual formation, not as a single hero's achievement. For broad fields, present an origin window, formation pressure, or early turn. Do not imply that one scene created the entire discipline.

## First-Pass Workflow

For Subject Origin pages, do not generate or assign images in the first writing pass.

Use this sequence:

1. Generate article text first.
2. Review the article for origin problem, old question, friction, new question, core knowledge turn, later branches, readability, and section clarity.
3. Modify the article until it passes.
4. Only after the article is approved, optionally generate visual suggestions.
5. Visual suggestions should not block article approval.

Reason: images should support the final knowledge turn, sequence, contrast, or relationship. If images are selected too early, they may become decorative, misleading, or mismatched after the article changes.

## First-Pass Output Format

In the first pass, output only:

```text
学科起源
{subject}

一句简介
{30-50 Chinese characters}

起源故事
{500-800 Chinese characters}

为什么这个学科需要出现
{200-300 Chinese characters}

核心转向
{150-250 Chinese characters}

关键人物 / 事件 / 工具
{150-250 Chinese characters}

后来长出的分支
{150-250 Chinese characters}

反思问题 / 小测
{50-100 Chinese characters}
```

Do not include in the first pass:

- image prompts;
- visual module suggestions;
- illustration descriptions;
- layout notes;
- hero image text;
- timeline image prompts.

## Default Length

Use the standard version by default.

- Minimum usable version: 600-800 Chinese characters.
- Standard version: 900-1500 Chinese characters.
- Deep version: 1800-2500 Chinese characters.

Do not go below 800 Chinese characters for a full Subject Origin page unless the page is explicitly designed as a compact card.

Do not exceed 2000 Chinese characters on normal public pages unless the page uses strong sectioning, visual cards, timeline blocks, or progressive disclosure.

## Paragraphing And Scanability

Do not write the Subject Origin page as one continuous block.

Use short paragraphs within each required section:

- `起源故事`: normally 2-4 short paragraphs, each carrying one beat: scene/old question, friction, changed action, changed question/new seeing.
- `为什么这个学科需要出现`: 1-2 short paragraphs.
- `核心转向`: 1-2 short paragraphs, with a clear "从 X，转向 Y" structure.
- `关键人物 / 事件 / 工具`: 1-2 short paragraphs or a compact sentence chain, not a crowded name list.
- `后来长出的分支`: 1-2 short paragraphs, showing branch directions rather than dumping terms.

Avoid paragraphs longer than about 180-220 Chinese characters on normal public pages. Paragraph breaks should help scanning without turning the article into bullet notes.

## Required Content Structure

### 1. One-Sentence Entry

Length: 30-50 Chinese characters.

Create curiosity without reducing the field to a definition. It may name a concrete pressure, scene, puzzle, or old limit.

Do not reveal the whole abstract conclusion.

### 2. Origin Story

Length: 500-800 Chinese characters.

The story must include:

- scene;
- old question or old way of working;
- why the old way was reasonable;
- friction or evidence pressure;
- changed question;
- new way of seeing.

The story should be readable as a scene, not a mini encyclopedia. If the field is broad, choose one origin window or formation turn.

### 3. Why The Field Became Necessary

Length: 200-300 Chinese characters.

Explain what the older way of thinking or working could not handle. This section may use clearer explanatory language, but it should stay connected to the origin story.

### 4. Core Knowledge Turn

Length: 150-250 Chinese characters.

Use a clear structure:

```text
From X, toward Y.
```

In Chinese, prefer:

```text
从 X，转向 Y。
```

This should name the central question shift, not define the whole discipline.

### 5. Key People / Events / Tools

Length: 150-250 Chinese characters.

Include only the most relevant 2-3 anchors. These may be people, events, texts, tools, methods, institutions, diagrams, or practices.

Do not turn this into a biography list or chronology dump.

### 6. Later Branches

Length: 150-250 Chinese characters.

Show how the discipline later developed into several directions. Keep the branches connected to the original turn.

### 7. Reflection Question Or Mini Check

Length: 50-100 Chinese characters.

The question should connect the origin story to the reader's way of thinking. It should return to the core object, practice, or question shift.

## Web Reading Standard

Users often scan web pages rather than read word by word. Treat this as a design warning: do not rely on one long text block.

Use:

- clear sections;
- short paragraphs;
- scannable cards;
- concrete section titles;
- one idea per block.

Avoid:

- one long text wall;
- Wikipedia-style accumulation;
- overloading the page with historical detail;
- opening with a definition.

## Visual Stage After Approval

A Subject Origin article can pass without images.

Images are optional and should be added only after the article text passes review and the structure is stable.

Bad or mismatched images are worse than no images. Do not use images as decoration.

After approval, optional visual suggestions may include:

1. Origin scene visual
2. Key object / tool / practice visual
3. Old question -> new question diagram
4. Later branches map
5. Reflection or mini-quiz card

Each visual suggestion must explain:

- what part of the article it supports;
- why it helps understanding;
- what should not be shown.

Visuals may be illustrations, diagrams, cards, timelines, maps, object close-ups, or symbolic scenes. They do not need to be photographs.

## Visual Quality Standard

Use images and diagrams only when they support understanding. Visuals should explain structure, sequence, contrast, or relationships.

A visual is useful when it helps the reader see:

- the historical or conceptual situation where the field became necessary;
- the object, tool, text, method, institution, diagram, or practice that helped the field emerge;
- the old question -> new question shift;
- later branches growing from the origin.

Do not use visuals merely for atmosphere, decoration, or page filling.

## Page Design Rule

Use one main idea per visual block.

Preferred rhythm after text approval:

```text
short text section -> visual card or diagram -> short text section -> visual card or timeline
```

Do not let visuals interrupt the article before the knowledge structure is stable.

## Quality Gate

A Subject Origin page can pass only if it answers:

1. What problem made this field necessary?
2. What older way of thinking or working was not enough?
3. What changed in the central question?
4. What object, method, tool, event, or practice made the change visible?
5. What later branches grew from this origin?
6. Are visuals absent in the first pass, or, if this is a later visual stage, do the visuals explain structure rather than decorate?

Fail the page if:

- it only defines the discipline;
- it only tells history without showing the knowledge turn;
- it treats one person or one discovery as the entire origin of a broad field;
- it has no clear old question -> new question shift;
- it depends on images to make the article understandable;
- it includes first-pass image prompts, illustration descriptions, hero image notes, or visual modules before the article has passed review;
- it uses images only for atmosphere or decoration in the visual stage.
