# Approved Story Quality Patterns

Use this reference for MapKAI historical discovery stories, field-origin stories, and integrated reader-facing knowledge stories.

## Pattern: Object-Based Discovery

A strong MapKAI story often works through one concrete object that changes what can be asked.

Example object types:

- map;
- pump handle;
- ledger;
- graph;
- seedling;
- water bucket;
- microscope lens;
- air sample;
- notebook;
- classroom wall;
- bridge model;
- hospital chart.

A story is stronger when the key object is not decoration but method.

The object should:

1. appear early;
2. be handled or used by someone;
3. make the old question insufficient;
4. help produce the new question;
5. return in the final reflection or final image.

## Pattern: Reasonable Old Explanation

The old explanation must not be mocked. It should first feel useful or plausible.

Weak:

```text
People wrongly believed bad air caused cholera.
```

Better:

```text
Bad air seemed plausible because the streets smelled of sewage, the city was crowded, and the sick often lived near the dirtiest places.
```

## Pattern: Evidence Pressure

Evidence pressure must be visible. It cannot be announced.

Weak:

```text
The evidence challenged the old theory.
```

Better:

```text
The black dots were not spread with the wind. They tightened around one pump. And the exceptions mattered: some nearby people stayed well because they drank other water; some farther people fell ill because they used that pump.
```

## Pattern: Changed Question

The story must change the question.

Weak:

```text
People began to understand disease differently.
```

Better:

```text
Instead of asking only where the air was worst, he began asking where the sick had drawn water.
```

## Pattern: Cautious Historical Support

Do not overclaim.

Use:

- "helped show";
- "became an important early case";
- "is often associated with";
- "contributed to";
- "supported the argument that".

Avoid:

- "proved forever";
- "single-handedly created";
- "for the first time in history";
- "immediately solved".
